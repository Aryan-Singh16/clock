Four Seasons of Hyderabad

Hyderabad follows a distinct 4-season pattern shaped by the southwest monsoon, continental air masses, and its Deccan Plateau elevation (~536m).

The most punishing season. May is the definitive peak — temperatures regularly cross 40°C and can reach 44–45°C during heatwave episodes. April sees the lowest humidity of the year (37%), making intense heat slightly more bearable. Pre-monsoon thunderstorms bring brief relief in May. Heatwave alerts from IMD are common April–May for Telangana. The hot season officially runs March 31 – June 3 (daily high above 37°C).

The SW Monsoon enters Hyderabad typically on June 8–12 (has been delayed up to 12 days). Rainfall surges from 36mm in May to 104mm in June, 170mm in July, 179mm in August. Over 73% of annual rainfall arrives in these 4 months. Low-lying areas flood (Pillar 191, Zoo Park underpasses). IMD issues Orange and Yellow alerts regularly. Bay of Bengal low-pressure systems amplify rainfall, occasionally triggering 100mm+ events in 24 hours.

A pleasant transition period. October sees temperatures rising again post-monsoon (29–32°C) with sporadic NE monsoon rains. This is peak cyclone season — Bay of Bengal cyclones can bring isolated heavy rainfall to Hyderabad. The devastating October 2020 floods (32cm in 24hrs, ₹1,230 crore losses) occurred in this period. November is generally dry and comfortable — ideal for travel.

Hyderabad's most comfortable season. December is coldest with nights dropping to 14–15°C (occasionally lower during Western Disturbance events). Days remain warm and pleasant (28°C). Virtually no rain. February has maximum sunshine hours (9.6 hrs/day). Cool season officially runs October 19 – January 30 (daily high below 29°C). Mild fog occurs in December–January mornings.

Why HITEC City / Gachibowli Feels Hotter

Modern IT towers feature reflective glass exteriors that don't absorb sunlight — instead bouncing solar radiation onto surrounding roads and pedestrians. Between 12 PM–4 PM, these buildings act like solar mirrors, intensifying local temperature by 3–5°C above official IMD readings. Unlike older Hyderabad localities with tree canopy and older construction, the Gachibowli-Madhapur-HITEC corridor is characterised by glass-steel structures, wide unshaded roads, and minimal green cover. Large vehicle density (IT commuters) also generates additional heat through exhaust and braking.

Flooding Risk in Monsoon Season

While Gachibowli itself sits on elevated terrain, surrounding low-lying areas flood severely during peak monsoon. GHMC flagged six high-impact zones: Khairatabad, LB Nagar, Charminar, Secunderabad, Kukatpally, and Serilingampally (which includes the Gachibowli area). In July 2025, parts of Hyderabad recorded 100+mm in 24 hours triggering traffic jams and power outages. The Pillar 191 underpass near Aramghar is among the most dangerous flood-prone spots near the IT corridor. HYDRAA and GHMC deploy emergency teams in this zone annually from June.

Key risk window: late July–August during active low-pressure systems. October cyclone window: monitor Bay of Bengal for post-monsoon systems.

IMD's MMCFS model suggests El Niño developing in Pacific — neutral ENSO currently but warming expected by mid-2026. Positive IOD developing toward late monsoon season could partially counteract El Niño's drying effect for South India / Telangana specifically. La Niña officially declared "Final Advisory" — transitioning to neutral/El Niño Watch. This combination explains below-normal national forecast but near-normal outlook for Telangana.

IMD Alert System

Understanding Hyderabad's Weather Alerts

The India Meteorological Department issues colour-coded alerts for Hyderabad and Telangana. During monsoon season (June–September), these activate frequently.

Red Alert
Extreme Rainfall / Heatwave
Rainfall exceeding 204.5mm in 24 hours. Or temperature 6°C+ above normal with severe heatwave. Take shelter; avoid outdoor movement. GHMC & SDRF on full deployment. Gachibowli underpasses closed during events. Very rare but triggered during Bay of Bengal deep depressions.
Orange Alert
Very Heavy Rain (115–204mm)
Common in July–August peak monsoon. Covers Hyderabad, Medchal-Malkajgiri, Rangareddy districts. Thunderstorms with lightning and 30–40 km/h gusts. Traffic disruptions in waterlogged low-lying zones. 3-hour advance warning via IMD Mausam app.
Yellow Alert
Moderate to Heavy Rain (64–115mm)
Most frequently issued alert for Hyderabad. Applies to Nalgonda, Medchal-Malkajgiri, Rangareddy, Vikarabad, Sangareddy. Caution during commute; avoid low-lying zones. Standard advisory: avoid open areas during lightning. IMD Damini app gives lightning 1km radius alerts.
